Chili Tofu With Rice

Description

Say it with me, with your chest:

Chili

Tofu

Rice.

I'm not going to try to explain this to you. No one could. It's just absurdly delicious. Trust me, and try it. You'll die.

Ingredients

The tofu

  • 1 block of soft tofu
  • kosher salt
  • beansprouts (optional)

The dipping sauce

  • 1 tbsp chili oil or Lao Gan Ma
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp msg
  • 1/4 tsp sichuan pepper
  • ~1 tbsp cilantro
  • 1/4 tsp sesame

The celery

  • 3 stalks celery
  • 1 tbsp chili oil or Lao Gan Ma
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp dark chinese vinegar
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/8 tsp msg
  • ~1/4 tsp toasted sesame oil

Instructions

You need to get four things ready: Your tofu, your dipping sauce, and your celery.

For the celery:

This is best done ahead of time.

  1. Peel your stalks of celery, split them in half, and cut into 1.5" sections.
  2. Blanch in boiling water for 30 seconds, then rinse under cold water.
  3. In a mason jar, mix the ingredients for the celery sauce.
  4. Chuck in the celery. No need to mix it now. Leave that in the fridge until you're ready to eat. When that time comes, shake it up and begin to munch.

For the dipping sauce:

All you need to do is mix the ingredients.

You can do this right before eating, or it can be handy to make this ahead of time as well, especially if you're packing lunch for work.

For the tofu:

You're ready for the home stretch.

  1. 20 minutes or so before lunch, you will put a block of tofu in a glass container, accompanied by a teaspon of salt, and cover it with hot water from your kettle. Start some rice now. (If you didn't do it before, mix your sauce up now while you wait.)
  2. After the 20 minutes, drain the water, place a bed of beansprouts underneath your tofu if you've got it, and fill with more boiled water. It's now ready to eat with your dipping sauce.

You did it

Get yourself a little bowl of your rice. Get some tofu -- delicately, delicately! The block is fragile. Dip that in the chili sauce. And, well, eat it. For many of us, this dish will develop your chopstick proficiency; embrace the challenge.

If you don't think that's delicious, I don't like you.
